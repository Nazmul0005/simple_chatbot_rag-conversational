3. MEDICATION TREATMENT
3.1 Overview: Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)
Definition: Use of medications in combination with counseling and behavioral therapies
Approach: Whole-patient treatment of substance use disorders
Approval: FDA-approved medications
3.2 Opioid Treatment Options
3.2.1 Acamprosate
Type: Opioid agonist
Function: Reduces opioid cravings and withdrawal symptoms
Distribution: Dispensed only through SAMHSA-certified opioid treatment programs (OTPs)
Dosing: Daily dosing typically required
3.2.2 Buprenorphine
Type: Partial opioid agonist
Function: Reduces cravings and withdrawal symptoms
Prescribers: Qualified physicians, nurse practitioners, and physician assistants with waiver
Forms: Tablets, film, or extended-release injection
3.2.3 Naltrexone
Type: Opioid antagonist
Function: Blocks euphoric effects of opioids
Properties: Non-addictive, does not lead to physical dependence
Forms: Tablets or extended-release injectable
3.3 Alcohol Treatment Options
3.3.1 Acamprosate (Alcohol)
Type: Opioid agonist
Function: Reduces opioid cravings and withdrawal symptoms
Distribution: Dispensed only through SAMHSA-certified opioid treatment programs (OTPs)
Dosing: Daily dosing typically required
3.3.2 Disulfiram
Function: Causes unpleasant reactions when alcohol is consumed
Mechanism: Acts as deterrent by making person feel sick if they consume alcohol
3.3.3 Naltrexone (Alcohol)
Function: Blocks pleasurable effects of alcohol and reduces cravings
Forms: Daily oral tablet or monthly injection
Property: Does not cause withdrawal if person drinks while taking it
3.4 Tobacco/Nicotine Treatment Options
3.4.1 Bupropion
Function: Helps reduce cravings and withdrawal symptoms
Type: Antidepressant medication
Purpose: Help people quit smoking
Form: Extended-release prescription medication
3.4.2 Varenicline
Function: Reduces both pleasurable effects of nicotine and withdrawal symptoms
Mechanism: Partially activates nicotine receptors while blocking nicotine from binding
Type: Prescription medication
3.4.3 Nicotine Replacement Therapies (NRT)
Function: Provides nicotine without harmful chemicals found in tobacco
Forms: Patches, gum, lozenges, inhalers, nasal sprays
Availability: Some over-the-counter, others require prescription
3.5 Stimulants (Cocaine/Methamphetamine) Treatment Options
3.5.1 Current Research Status
Status: No FDA-approved medications specifically for stimulant use disorder currently exist
Research: Ongoing studies
Primary Treatment: Behavioral therapies remain primary approach
3.5.2 Off-Label Options
Status: Some medications prescribed off-label for symptom management
Examples: Modafinil, bupropion, certain antidepressants
Note: Not FDA approved specifically for stimulant use disorder

5. COPING STRATEGIES
5.1 Mindfulness Strategies
5.1.1 Focus on Your Breath
Technique: Deep breathing exercises
Purpose: Reduce stress and anxiety
Method: Breathe in slowly for count of 4, hold for 1 second, breathe out for count of 5
5.1.2 Practice Grounding Techniques
Technique: 5-4-3-2-1 method
Use: When feeling overwhelmed
Method: Identify 5 things you see, 4 things you can touch, 3 things you hear, 2 things you smell, 1 thing you taste
5.2 Cognitive Strategies
5.2.1 Manage Negative Thoughts
Technique: Challenge negative self-talk
Method: Question accuracy and replace with balanced thoughts
5.2.2 Set Realistic Goals
Approach: Break down wellness goals into small, achievable steps
Focus: Rather than focusing on never using again
5.3 Behavioral Strategies
5.3.1 Delay and Distract
Method: Set timer for 20 minutes before acting on craving
Activities: Walking, calling friend, listening to music
5.3.2 Identify Triggers
Method: Keep journal of when cravings occur
Track: Specific people, places, emotions, or situations
5.4 Wellness & Social Strategies
5.4.1 Build a Support Network
Method: Connect with others who understand
Options: Support groups, trusted friends, family members
5.4.2 Practice Self-Care
Focus: Adequate sleep, nutrition, physical activity
Benefit: Reduce stress and cravings

6. TIPS & STRATEGIES
6.1 Quick Tips
6.1.1 Dealing with Cravings
Technique: 5-minute rule
Method: Wait 5 minutes before giving in
Rationale: Most cravings fade in this time
6.1.2 Urge Surfing
Approach: Notice urge without judgment
Visualization: Imagine as wave that rises, peaks, and eventually subsides
6.1.3 Replacement Activities
Method: Immediately substitute healthy activity when urge arises
Examples: Walking, calling friend
6.1.4 Habit Stacking
Method: Link new healthy habit with existing one
Example: "After I brush my teeth, I'll meditate for 2 minutes"
6.1.5 Environment Design
Method: Remove triggers from environment
Example: If alcohol is challenge, don't keep it at home
6.2 SAMHSA Strategies
6.2.1 Play the Tape Through (Thinking Strategy)
Method: Think about entire experience of acting on feelings
Focus: Not just immediate relief, but how you'll feel afterwards
Consider: Potential regret or disappointment
6.2.2 HALT Check-in (Behavioral Strategy)
Method: Ask if you're Hungry, Angry, Lonely, or Tired
Rationale: These states often trigger urges
Action: Address underlying need with healthy choices
6.2.3 DEADS Strategy (Behavioral Strategy)
Components: Delay, Escape, Avoid, Distract, Substitute
Purpose: Multiple approaches to manage urges
6.2.4 Mindfulness Practice (Thinking Strategy)
Method: Observe feeling without judgment
Visualization: Like wave that rises, peaks, and subsides on its own
6.2.5 Build Wellness Support (Social Strategy)
Focus: Continuously strengthen support systems
Elements: Coping skills and resources
Purpose: Maintain wellness and make healthy choices with urges

7. DETAILED TECHNIQUES
7.1 Urge Surfing Technique
Developer
Creator: Dr. Alan Marlatt
Purpose
Goal: Manage cravings through mindfulness
Steps
Notice when experiencing an urge
Instead of resisting or acting, observe it with curiosity
Pay attention to physical sensations in body
Breathe deeply and imagine urge as wave
The wave will rise, peak, and eventually subside on its own
Continue to breathe and observe as intensity decreases
Key Quote
"Cravings are like ocean waves: they grow in intensity, peak, and eventually subside. Your job is not to fight the wave but to ride it out."
7.2 DEADS Strategy Explained
D - Delay
Action: Postpone decision to use for 15 minutes or more
Purpose: Give craving time to pass
E - Escape
Action: Leave high-risk situation or environment
Purpose: Remove from triggering environment
A - Avoid
Action: Identify and stay away from trigger people, places, and things
Purpose: Prevent craving activation
D - Distract
Action: Engage in alternative activity
Purpose: Take mind off craving
S - Substitute
Action: Use healthy alternative
Purpose: Satisfy underlying need
7.3 HALT Method
Purpose
Use: Check state before acting on craving
H - Hungry
Issue: Physical hunger can be mistaken for cravings
Solution: Try eating nutritious meal first
A - Angry
Issue: Emotional distress triggering urge
Solution: Process emotions through journaling, talking to someone, or physical activity
L - Lonely
Issue: Social isolation triggering urge
Solution: Reach out to supportive friend, family member, or recovery community
T - Tired
Issue: Physical exhaustion triggering urge
Solution: Rest, take nap, or practice relaxation techniques to restore energy
